content: "<p><strong>Las Vegas</strong> (<span class=\"rt-commentedText nowrap\"><small><a title=\"American English\" href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/American_English\">US</a>: </small><span class=\"IPA nopopups noexcerpt\" lang=\"en-fonipa\"><a title=\"Help:IPA/English\" href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Help:IPA/English\">/<span style=\"border-bottom: 1px dotted;\"><span title=\"'l' in 'lie'\">l</span><span title=\"/ɑː/: 'a' in 'father'\">ɑː</span><span title=\"'s' in 'sigh'\">s</span><span title=\"/ˈ/: primary stress follows\">ˈ</span><span title=\"'v' in 'vie'\">v</span><span title=\"/eɪ/: 'a' in 'face'\">eɪ</span><span title=\"/ɡ/: 'g' in 'guy'\">ɡ</span><span title=\"/ə/: 'a' in 'about'\">ə</span><span title=\"'s' in 'sigh'\">s</span></span>/</a></span></span>; <a title=\"Spanish language\" href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanish_language\">Spanish</a> for \"The Meadows\"), often known simply as <strong>Vegas</strong>, is the <a title=\"List of United States cities by population\" href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_United_States_cities_by_population\">25th-most populous city</a> in the United States, the most populous city in the <a title=\"U.S. state\" href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/U.S._state\">state</a> of <a title=\"Nevada\" href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nevada\">Nevada</a>, and the <a title=\"County seat\" href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/County_seat\">county seat</a> of <a title=\"Clark County, Nevada\" href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Clark_County,_Nevada\">Clark County</a>. The <a title=\"Las Vegas Valley\" href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Las_Vegas_Valley\">Las Vegas Valley</a> metropolitan area is the largest within the greater <a title=\"Mojave Desert\" href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mojave_Desert\">Mojave Desert</a>, and 2nd-largest in the <a title=\"Southwestern United States\" href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Southwestern_United_States\">Southwestern United States</a>.<sup id=\"cite_ref-GR6_6-0\" class=\"reference\"><a href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Las_Vegas#cite_note-GR6-6\">[6]</a></sup><sup id=\"cite_ref-Brunn_Zeigler_Hays-Mitchell_Graybill_2020_p._29_7-0\" class=\"reference\"><a href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Las_Vegas#cite_note-Brunn_Zeigler_Hays-Mitchell_Graybill_2020_p._29-7\">[7]</a></sup> Las Vegas is an internationally renowned major <a title=\"Resort town\" href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Resort_town\">resort city</a>, known primarily for its <a title=\"Gambling\" href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gambling\">gambling</a>, shopping, fine dining, entertainment, and <a title=\"Nightlife\" href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nightlife\">nightlife</a>. The Las Vegas Valley as a whole serves as the leading financial, commercial, and cultural center for Nevada.</p>\r\n<p>The city bills itself as <a title=\"The Entertainment Capital of the World\" href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Entertainment_Capital_of_the_World\">the Entertainment Capital of the World</a>, and is famous for its luxurious and extremely large casino-hotels. With over 2.9 million visitors as of 2019, Las Vegas is the sixth-most visited city in the United States after <a title=\"New York City\" href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_York_City\">New York City</a>, <a title=\"Miami\" href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Miami\">Miami</a>, <a title=\"Los Angeles\" href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Los_Angeles\">Los Angeles</a>, <a class=\"mw-redirect\" title=\"Orlando\" href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Orlando\">Orlando</a>, and <a title=\"San Francisco\" href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/San_Francisco\">San Francisco</a>.<sup id=\"cite_ref-8\" class=\"reference\"><a href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Las_Vegas#cite_note-8\">[8]</a></sup> It is a top three destination in the United States for business conventions and a global leader in the <a title=\"Hospitality industry\" href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hospitality_industry\">hospitality industry</a>, claiming more <a class=\"mw-redirect\" title=\"AAA Five Diamond Award\" href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/AAA_Five_Diamond_Award\">AAA Five Diamond</a> hotels than any other city in the world.<sup id=\"cite_ref-9\" class=\"reference\"><a href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Las_Vegas#cite_note-9\">[9]</a></sup><sup id=\"cite_ref-10\" class=\"reference\"><a href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Las_Vegas#cite_note-10\">[10]</a></sup><sup id=\"cite_ref-11\" class=\"reference\"><a href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Las_Vegas#cite_note-11\">[11]</a></sup> Today, Las Vegas annually ranks as one of the world's most visited tourist destinations.<sup id=\"cite_ref-TravelandTourism_12-0\" class=\"reference\"><a href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Las_Vegas#cite_note-TravelandTourism-12\">[12]</a></sup><sup id=\"cite_ref-13\" class=\"reference\"><a href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Las_Vegas#cite_note-13\">[13]</a></sup> The city's tolerance for numerous forms of adult entertainment earned it the title of \"<a title=\"Sin City (description)\" href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sin_City_(description)\">Sin City</a>\",<sup id=\"cite_ref-14\" class=\"reference\"><a href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Las_Vegas#cite_note-14\">[14]</a></sup> and has made Las Vegas a popular setting for literature, films, television programs, and music videos.</p>\r\n<p>Las Vegas was settled in 1905 and officially incorporated in 1911. At the close of the 20th century, it was the most populated North American city founded within that century (a similar distinction was earned by <a title=\"Chicago\" href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chicago\">Chicago</a> in the 19th century). Population growth has accelerated since the 1960s, and between 1990 and 2000 the population nearly doubled, increasing by 85.2%. Rapid growth has continued into the 21st century, and according to the <a title=\"United States Census Bureau\" href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States_Census_Bureau\">United States Census Bureau</a>, the city had 641,903 residents in 2020,<sup id=\"cite_ref-QuickFacts_15-0\" class=\"reference\"><a href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Las_Vegas#cite_note-QuickFacts-15\">[15]</a></sup> with a metropolitan population of 2,227,053.<sup id=\"cite_ref-Census_2010_16-0\" class=\"reference\"><a href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Las_Vegas#cite_note-Census_2010-16\">[16]</a></sup></p>\r\n<p>As with most major metropolitan areas, the name of the primary city (\"Las Vegas\" in this case) is often used to describe areas beyond official city limits. In the case of Las Vegas, this especially applies to the areas on and near the <a title=\"Las Vegas Strip\" href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Las_Vegas_Strip\">Las Vegas Strip</a>, which are actually located within the unincorporated communities of <a title=\"Paradise, Nevada\" href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paradise,_Nevada\">Paradise</a> and <a title=\"Winchester, Nevada\" href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Winchester,_Nevada\">Winchester</a>.<sup id=\"cite_ref-17\" class=\"reference\"><a href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Las_Vegas#cite_note-17\">[17]</a></sup><sup id=\"cite_ref-18\" class=\"reference\"><a href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Las_Vegas#cite_note-18\">[18]</a></sup> Nevada is the driest state, and Las Vegas is the driest major U.S. city. Over time and influenced by <a title=\"Climate change\" href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_change\">climate change</a>, <a title=\"Drought\" href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Drought\">droughts</a> in Southern Nevada have been increasing in frequency and severity,<sup id=\"cite_ref-19\" class=\"reference\"><a href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Las_Vegas#cite_note-19\">[19]</a></sup> putting a further strain on Las Vegas' <a title=\"Water security\" href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Water_security\">water security</a>.</p>\r\n<h2><span id=\"Toponymy\" class=\"mw-headline\">Toponymy</span></h2>\r\n<p>The area was named Las Vegas, which is behind petroglyphs. <a class=\"mw-redirect\" title=\"Ancient Pueblo peoples\" href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ancient_Pueblo_peoples\">Ancient Puebloan</a> and <a class=\"mw-redirect\" first non-<a title=\"Native Americans in the United States\" href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Native_Americans_in_the_United_States\">Native American</a> to encounter the valley, in 1829.<sup 1829.<sup id=\"cite_ref-Clark_County_23-0\" class=\"reference\"><a href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Las_Vegas#cite_note-Clark_County-23\">[23]</a></sup><sup id=\"cite_ref-ponce_24-0\" Downtown Las Vegas's Fremont Street is named after him.</p>\r\n<p>Eleven years later, members of <a title=\"The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints\" href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Church_of_Jesus_Christ_of_Latter-day_Saints\">the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints</a> chose Las Vegas as the site to build a fort halfway between <a title=\"Salt Lake City\" href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Salt_Lake_City\">Salt Lake City</a> and Los Angeles, where they would travel to gather supplies. The fort was abandoned several years afterward. The remainder of this <a title=\"Old Las Vegas Mormon Fort State Historic Park\" href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Old_Las_Vegas_Mormon_Fort_State_Historic_Park\">Old Mormon Fort</a> can still be seen at the intersection of Las Vegas Boulevard and Washington Avenue.</p>\r\n<p>Las Vegas was founded as a city in 1905, when 110 acres (45&nbsp;ha) of land adjacent to the <a title=\"Union Pacific Railroad\" href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Union_Pacific_Railroad\">Union Pacific Railroad</a> tracks were auctioned in what would become the downtown area. At that time, Nevada legalized casino gambling and reduced residency requirements for divorce to six weeks. This year also witnessed the beginning of construction on nearby <a title=\"Hoover Dam\" href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hoover_Dam\">Hoover Dam</a>. The influx of construction workers and their families helped Las Vegas avoid economic calamity during the Great Depression. The construction work was completed in 1935.</p>\r\n<p>In late 1941, <a title=\"Nellis Air Force Base\" href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nellis_Air_Force_Base#History\">Las Vegas Army Airfield</a> was established. Renamed <a title=\"Nellis Air Force Base\" href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nellis_Air_Force_Base\">Nellis Air Force Base</a> in 1950, it is now home to the <a title=\"United States Air Force Thunderbirds\" href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States_Air_Force_Thunderbirds\">United States Air Force Thunderbirds</a> aerobatic team.<sup id=\"cite_ref-26\" class=\"reference\"><a href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Las_Vegas#cite_note-26\">[26]</a></sup></p>\r\n<p>Following World War II, lavishly decorated hotels, gambling casinos, and big-name entertainment became synonymous with Las Vegas.</p>\r\n<div class=\"thumb tright\">\r\n<div class=\"thumbinner\" style=\"width: 222px;\"><a class=\"image\" href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:NNSA-NSO-787.jpg\"><img class=\"thumbimage\" src=\"https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/eb/NNSA-NSO-787.jpg/220px-NNSA-NSO-787.jpg\" srcset=\"//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/eb/NNSA-NSO-787.jpg/330px-NNSA-NSO-787.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/e/eb/NNSA-NSO-787.jpg 2x\" width=\"220\" height=\"174\" data-file-width=\"360\" data-file-height=\"284\"></a>\r\n<div class=\"thumbcaption\">\r\n<div class=\"magnify\">&nbsp;</div>\r\n</div>\r\n</div>\r\n</div>\r\n<div class=\"thumb tright\">\r\n<div class=\"thumbinner\" style=\"width: 222px;\">\r\n<div class=\"thumbcaption\">This view of downtown Las Vegas shows a <a title=\"Mushroom cloud\" href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mushroom_cloud\">mushroom cloud</a> in the background. situated within <a title=\"Clark County, Nevada\" href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Clark_County,_Nevada\">Clark County</a>, in a <a title=\"Basin and range topography\" href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Basin_and_range_topography\">basin</a> on the floor of the <a title=\"Mojave Desert\" href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mojave_Desert\">Mojave Desert</a>,<sup Much of the landscape is rocky and arid, with desert vegetation and wildlife. It can be subjected to torrential flash floods, although much has been done to mitigate the effects of flash floods through improved drainage systems.<sup id=\"cite_ref-31\" class=\"reference\"><a href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Las_Vegas#cite_note-31\">[31]</a></sup></p>\r\n<p>The peaks surrounding Las Vegas reach elevations of over 10,000 feet (3,000&nbsp;m), and act as barriers to the strong flow of moisture from the surrounding area. The elevation is approximately 2,030&nbsp;ft (620&nbsp;m) above sea level. According to the <a title=\"United States Census Bureau\" href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States_Census_Bureau\">United States Census Bureau</a>, the city has a total area of 135.86&nbsp;sq&nbsp;mi (351.9&nbsp;km<sup>2</sup>), of which 135.81&nbsp;sq&nbsp;mi (351.7&nbsp;km<sup>2</sup>) is land and 0.05&nbsp;sq&nbsp;mi (0.13&nbsp;km<sup>2</sup>) (0.03%) is water.</p>\r\n<p>After Alaska and California, Nevada is the third most seismically active state in the U.S. It has been estimated by the United States Geological Survey (USGS) that over the next 50 years, there is a 10&ndash;20% chance of an M6.0 or greater earthquake occurring within 50&nbsp;km (31&nbsp;mi) of Las Vegas.<sup id=\"cite_ref-32\" class=\"reference\"><a href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Las_Vegas#cite_note-32\">[32]</a></sup></p>\r\n<p>Within the city, there are many lawns, trees and other greenery. Due to water resource issues, there has been a movement to encourage <a title=\"Xeriscaping\" href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Xeriscaping\">xeriscapes</a>. Another part of conservation efforts is scheduled watering days for residential landscaping. A <a class=\"mw-redirect\" title=\"U.S. Environmental Protection Agency\" href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/U.S._Environmental_Protection_Agency\">U.S. Environmental Protection Agency</a> grant in <a class=\"mw-redirect\" title=\"Subtropical climate\" href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Subtropical_climate\">subtropical</a> <a class=\"mw-redirect\" title=\"Hot desert climate\" href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hot_desert_climate\">hot desert climate</a> (<a title=\"K&ouml;ppen climate classification\" href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/K%C3%B6ppen_climate_classification\">K&ouml;ppen climate classification</a>: <em>BWh</em>), typical of the <a title=\"Mojave Desert\" href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mojave_Desert\">Mojave Desert</a> in which it lies. This climate is typified by long, extremely hot summers; warm transitional seasons; and short winters with mild days and cool nights. There is abundant sunshine throughout the year, with an average of 310 sunny days and bright sunshine during 86% of all daylight hours.<sup id=\"cite_ref-NOWData_NWS_Las_Vegas,_NV_(VEF)_-_LASthr_33-0\" class=\"reference\"><a href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Las_Vegas#cite_note-NOWData_NWS_Las_Vegas,_NV_(VEF)_-_LASthr-33\">[33]</a></sup><sup id=\"cite_ref-noaasun_34-0\" class=\"reference\"><a href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Las_Vegas#cite_note-noaasun-34\">[34]</a></sup> Rainfall is scarce, with an average of 4.2&nbsp;in (110&nbsp;mm) dispersed between roughly 26 total rainy days per year.<sup id=\"cite_ref-NCEI_Summary_of_Monthly_Normals_-_1991-2020_35-0\" class=\"reference\"><a href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Las_Vegas#cite_note-NCEI_Summary_of_Monthly_Normals_-_1991-2020-35\">[35]</a></sup> Las Vegas is among the sunniest, driest, and least humid locations in North America, with exceptionally low dew points and humidity that sometimes remains below 10%.<sup id=\"cite_ref-36\" class=\"reference\"><a href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Las_Vegas#cite_note-36\">[36]</a></sup></p>\r\n<p>The summer months of June through September are extremely hot, though moderated by extremely low humidity. July is the hottest month, with an average daytime high of 104.5&nbsp;&deg;F (40.3&nbsp;&deg;C). On average, 137 days per year reach or exceed 90&nbsp;&deg;F (32&nbsp;&deg;C), of which 78 days reach 100&nbsp;&deg;F (38&nbsp;&deg;C) and 10 days reach 110&nbsp;&deg;F (43&nbsp;&deg;C). During the peak intensity of summer, overnight lows frequently remain above 80&nbsp;&deg;F (27&nbsp;&deg;C), and occasionally above 85&nbsp;&deg;F (29&nbsp;&deg;C).<sup id=\"cite_ref-NOWData_NWS_Las_Vegas,_NV_(VEF)_-_LASthr_33-1\" class=\"reference\"><a href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Las_Vegas#cite_note-NOWData_NWS_Las_Vegas,_NV_(VEF)_-_LASthr-33\">[33]</a></sup></p>\r\n<p>While most summer days are consistently hot, dry, and cloudless, the <a class=\"mw-redirect\" title=\"North American Monsoon\" href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/North_American_Monsoon\">North American Monsoon</a> sporadically interrupts this pattern and brings more cloud cover, thunderstorms, lightning, increased humidity, and brief spells of heavy rain. Potential monsoons affect Las Vegas between July and August. Summer in Las Vegas is marked by a significant <a class=\"mw-redirect\" title=\"Diurnal temperature variation\" href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Diurnal_temperature_variation\">diurnal temperature variation</a>. While less extreme than other parts of the state, nighttime lows in Las Vegas are often 30&nbsp;&deg;F (16.7&nbsp;&deg;C) or more lower than daytime highs.<sup id=\"cite_ref-37\" class=\"reference\"><a href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Las_Vegas#cite_note-37\">[37]</a></sup> The average hottest night of the year is 90&nbsp;&deg;F (32&nbsp;&deg;C). The all-time record is at 95&nbsp;&deg;F (35&nbsp;&deg;C).<sup id=\"cite_ref-NOWData_NWS_Las_Vegas,_NV_(VEF)_-_LASthr_33-2\" class=\"reference\"><a href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Las_Vegas#cite_note-NOWData_NWS_Las_Vegas,_NV_(VEF)_-_LASthr-33\">[33]</a></sup></p>\r\n<p>Las Vegas winters are short and generally very mild, with chilly (but rarely cold) daytime temperatures. Sunshine is abundant in all seasons. December is both the year's coolest and cloudiest month, with an average daytime high of 56.9&nbsp;&deg;F (13.8&nbsp;&deg;C) and sunshine occurring during 78% of its daylight hours. Winter evenings are defined by clear skies and swift drops in temperature after sunset, with overnight minima averaging around 40&nbsp;&deg;F (4.4&nbsp;&deg;C) in December and January. Owing to its elevation that ranges from 2,000 to 3,000 feet (610 to 910&nbsp;m), Las Vegas experiences markedly cooler winters than other areas of the <a title=\"Mojave Desert\" href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mojave_Desert\">Mojave Desert</a> and the adjacent <a title=\"Sonoran Desert\" href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sonoran_Desert\">Sonoran Desert</a> that are closer to sea level. The city records freezing temperatures an average of 10 nights per winter. It is exceptionally rare for temperatures to reach or fall below 25&nbsp;&deg;F (&minus;4&nbsp;&deg;C).<sup id=\"cite_ref-NOWData_NWS_Las_Vegas,_NV_(VEF)_-_LASthr_33-3\" class=\"reference\"><a href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Las_Vegas#cite_note-NOWData_NWS_Las_Vegas,_NV_(VEF)_-_LASthr-33\">[33]</a></sup></p>\r\n<p>Most of the annual precipitation falls during the winter months. February, the wettest month, averages only four days of measurable rain. The mountains immediately surrounding the Las Vegas Valley accumulate snow every winter, but significant accumulation within the city is rare, although moderate accumulations occur every few years. The most recent accumulations occurred on February 18, 2019, when parts of the city received about 1 to 2 inches (2.5 to 5.1&nbsp;cm) of snow<sup id=\"cite_ref-38\" class=\"reference\"><a href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Las_Vegas#cite_note-38\">[38]</a></sup> and on February 20 when the city received almost 0.5 inches (1.3&nbsp;cm).<sup id=\"cite_ref-39\" class=\"reference\"><a href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Las_Vegas#cite_note-39\">[39]</a></sup> Other recent significant snow accumulations occurred on December 25, 2015, and December 17, 2008.<sup id=\"cite_ref-40\" class=\"reference\"><a href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Las_Vegas#cite_note-40\">[40]</a></sup> Unofficially, Las Vegas's largest snowfall on record was the 12 inches (30&nbsp;cm) that fell in 1909.<sup id=\"cite_ref-41\" class=\"reference\"><a href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Las_Vegas#cite_note-41\">[41]</a></sup> In recent times, ice days have not occurred, although 29&nbsp;&deg;F (&minus;2&nbsp;&deg;C) was measured in 1963.<sup id=\"cite_ref-NOWData_NWS_Las_Vegas,_NV_(VEF)_-_LASthr_33-4\" class=\"reference\"><a href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Las_Vegas#cite_note-NOWData_NWS_Las_Vegas,_NV_(VEF)_-_LASthr-33\">[33]</a></sup> On average the coldest day is 44&nbsp;&deg;F (7&nbsp;&deg;C).<sup id=\"cite_ref-NOWData_NWS_Las_Vegas,_NV_(VEF)_-_LASthr_33-5\" class=\"reference\"><a href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Las_Vegas#cite_note-NOWData_NWS_Las_Vegas,_NV_(VEF)_-_LASthr-33\">[33]</a></sup></p>\r\n<p>117&nbsp;&deg;F (47&nbsp;&deg;C) is the highest temperature officially observed for Las Vegas, as measured at <a title=\"Harry Reid International Airport\" href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Harry_Reid_International_Airport\">Harry Reid International Airport</a> on July 10, 2021, the last of five occasions.<sup id=\"cite_ref-NOWData_NWS_Las_Vegas,_NV_(VEF)_-_LASthr_33-6\" class=\"reference\"><a href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Las_Vegas#cite_note-NOWData_NWS_Las_Vegas,_NV_(VEF)_-_LASthr-33\">[33]</a></sup> The lowest temperature was 8&nbsp;&deg;F (&minus;13&nbsp;&deg;C), recorded on two days: January 25, 1937, and January 13, 1963.<sup id=\"cite_ref-NOWData_NWS_Las_Vegas,_NV_(VEF)_-_LASthr_33-7\" class=\"reference\"><a href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Las_Vegas#cite_note-NOWData_NWS_Las_Vegas,_NV_(VEF)_-_LASthr-33\">[33]</a></sup> The highest temperature ever measured within the city of Las Vegas was 118&nbsp;&deg;F (48&nbsp;&deg;C) on July 26, 1931.<sup id=\"cite_ref-42\" class=\"reference\"><a href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Las_Vegas#cite_note-42\">[42]</a></sup> The official record hot daily minimum is 95&nbsp;&deg;F (35&nbsp;&deg;C) on July 19, 2005, and July 1, 2013. The official record cold daily maximum is 28&nbsp;&deg;F (&minus;2&nbsp;&deg;C) on January 8 and 21, 1937.<sup id=\"cite_ref-NOWData_NWS_Las_Vegas,_NV_(VEF)_-_LASthr_33-8\" class=\"reference\"><a There were 190,724 housing units at an average density of 1,683.3/sq&nbsp;mi (649.9/km<sup>2</sup>).</p>\r\n<p>As of 2006, there were 176,750 households, of which 31.9% had children under age 18 living with them, 48.3% were married couples living together, 12.2% had a female householder with no husband present, and 33.5% were non-families. 25.0% of all households were made up of individuals, and 7.5% had someone living alone who was 65 years of age or older. The average household size was 2.66 and the average family size was 3.20.</p>\r\n<p>In the city, the population age distribution was as follows:</p>\r\n<ul>\r\n<li>25.9% under the age of 18</li>\r\n<li>8.8% from 18 to 24</li>\r\n<li>32.0% from 25 to 44</li>\r\n<li>21.7% from 45 to 64</li>\r\n<li>11.6% who were 65 years of age or older</li>\r\n</ul>\r\n<p>The median age was 34 years. For every 100 females, there were 103.3 males. For every 100 females age 18 and over, there were 102.5 males.</p>\r\n<p>The <a class=\"mw-redirect\" title=\"Median household income\" href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Median_household_income\">median income</a> for a household in the city was $53,000 and the median income for a family was $58,465.<sup id=\"cite_ref-59\" class=\"reference\"><a href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Las_Vegas#cite_note-59\">[57]</a></sup> Males had a median income of $35,511 versus $27,554 for females. The per capita income for the city was $22,060. About 6.6% of families and 8.9% of the population were below the poverty line, including 5.4% of those under age 18 and 6.3% of those age 65 or over.</p>\r\n<p>According to a 2004 study, Las Vegas has one of the highest divorce rates.<sup id=\"cite_ref-60\" class=\"reference\"><a href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Las_Vegas#cite_note-60\">[58]</a></sup><sup id=\"cite_ref-61\" class=\"reference\"><a href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Las_Vegas#cite_note-61\">[59]</a></sup> The city's high divorce rate is not wholly due to Las Vegans themselves getting divorced. Since divorce is easier in Nevada than in most other states, many people come from across the country for the easier process. Similarly, Nevada marriages are notoriously easy to get. Las Vegas has one of the highest marriage rates of U.S. cities, with many licenses issued to people from outside the area (see <a title=\"Las Vegas weddings\" This resulted in a drop in tourism in the downtown area, but many recent projects have increased the number of visitors to downtown.</p>\r\n<p>An effort has been made by city officials to diversify the economy by attracting health-related, high-tech and other commercial interests. No state tax for individuals or corporations, as well as a lack of other forms of business-related taxes, have aided the success of these efforts.<sup id=\"cite_ref-66\" class=\"reference\"><a href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Las_Vegas#cite_note-66\">[64]</a></sup></p>\r\n<p>The Fremont Street Experience was built in an effort to draw tourists back to the area and has been popular since its startup in 1995.</p>\r\n<p>The city purchased 61 acres (25&nbsp;ha) of property from the <a title=\"Union Pacific Railroad\" href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Union_Pacific_Railroad\">Union Pacific Railroad</a> in 1995 with the goal of creating a better draw for more people to the downtown area. In 2004, Las Vegas Mayor <a title=\"Oscar Goodman\" href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oscar_Goodman\">Oscar Goodman</a> announced plans for <a title=\"Symphony Park\" href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Symphony_Park\">Symphony Park</a>, which could include a mixture of offerings, such as residential space and office buildings.</p>\r\n<p>Already operating in Symphony Park is the Cleveland Clinic <a title=\"Lou Ruvo Center for Brain Health\" href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lou_Ruvo_Center_for_Brain_Health\">Lou Ruvo Center for Brain Health</a> (opened in 2010), <a class=\"mw-redirect\" title=\"The Smith Center for the Performing Arts\" href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Smith_Center_for_the_Performing_Arts\">The Smith Center for the Performing Arts</a> (opened in 2012) and the DISCOVERY Children's Museum (opened in 2013).<sup id=\"cite_ref-67\" class=\"reference\"><a href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Las_Vegas#cite_note-67\">[65]</a></sup></p>\r\n<p>On land across from Symphony Park, the <a class=\"mw-redirect\" title=\"World Market Center\" href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/World_Market_Center\">World Market Center</a> Las Vegas opened in 2005. It currently encompasses three large buildings with a total of 5.1&nbsp;million square feet. Trade shows for the furniture and furnishing industries are held there semiannually.</p>\r\n<p>Also located nearby is the Las Vegas North Premium Outlets. With a second expansion, completed in May 2015, the mall currently offers 175 stores.<sup id=\"cite_ref-68\" class=\"reference\"><a href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Las_Vegas#cite_note-68\">[66]</a></sup></p>\r\n<p>City offices moved to a new <a title=\"Las Vegas City Hall\" href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Las_Vegas_City_Hall\">Las Vegas City Hall</a> in February 2013 on downtown's Main Street. The former City Hall building is now occupied by the corporate headquarters for the major online retailer, <a class=\"mw-redirect\" title=\"Zappos.com\" href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zappos.com\">Zappos.com</a>, which opened downtown in 2013. Zappos CEO <a title=\"Tony Hsieh\" href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tony_Hsieh\">Tony Hsieh</a> took an interest in the urban area and contributed $350&nbsp;million toward a revitalization effort called the Downtown Project.<sup id=\"cite_ref-69\" class=\"reference\"><a href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Las_Vegas#cite_note-69\">[67]</a></sup><sup id=\"cite_ref-70\" class=\"reference\"><a href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Las_Vegas#cite_note-70\">[68]</a></sup> Projects funded include Las Vegas's first independent bookstore, <a title=\"The Writer's Block\" href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Writer%27s_Block\">The Writer's Block</a>.<sup id=\"cite_ref-71\" class=\"reference\"><a href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Las_Vegas#cite_note-71\">[69]</a></sup></p>\r\n<h3><span id=\"Other_industries\" class=\"mw-headline\">Other industries</span></h3>\r\n<p>A number of new industries have moved to Las Vegas in recent decades. The Mayor sits as a Council member-at-large and presides over all of the <a class=\"mw-redirect\" title=\"City council\" href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/City_council\">city council</a> meetings. If the Mayor cannot preside over a City Council meeting, then the Mayor Pro-Tem is the <a class=\"mw-redirect\" title=\"Chairman\" href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chairman\">presiding officer</a> of the meeting until the Mayor returns to his/her seat. The City Manager is responsible for the administration and the day-to-day operations of all <a title=\"Municipal services\" href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Municipal_services\">municipal services</a> and city departments. The City Manager maintains intergovernmental relationships with federal, state, county and other local governments.</p>\r\n<p>Much of the Las Vegas metropolitan area is split into neighboring incorporated cities or unincorporated communities. Approximately 700,000 people live in unincorporated areas governed by <a title=\"Clark County, Nevada\" href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Clark_County,_Nevada\">Clark County</a>, and another 465,000 live in incorporated cities such as <a title=\"North Las Vegas, Nevada\" href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/North_Las_Vegas,_Nevada\">North Las Vegas</a>, <a title=\"Henderson, Nevada\" href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Henderson,_Nevada\">Henderson</a> and <a title=\"Boulder City, Nevada\" href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Boulder_City,_Nevada\">Boulder City</a>. Las Vegas and Clark County share a police department, the <a title=\"Las Vegas Metropolitan Police Department\" href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Las_Vegas_Metropolitan_Police_Department\">Las Vegas Metropolitan Police Department</a>, which was formed after a 1973 merger of the <a class=\"mw-redirect\" title=\"Las Vegas Police Department (Nevada)\" href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Las_Vegas_Police_Department_(Nevada)\">Las Vegas Police Department</a> and the <a class=\"mw-redirect\" title=\"Clark County Sheriff's Department (Nevada)\" href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Clark_County_Sheriff%27s_Department_(Nevada)\">Clark County Sheriff's Department</a>. North Las Vegas, Henderson, Boulder City and some colleges have their own police departments.</p>\r\n<p>A <a class=\"mw-redirect\" title=\"Southern Paiute\" href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Southern_Paiute\">Paiute</a> <a title=\"Indian reservation\" href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indian_reservation\">Indian reservation</a> occupies about 1 acre (0.40&nbsp;ha) in the downtown area.</p>\r\n<p>Las Vegas, home to the <a class=\"mw-redirect\" title=\"U.S. District Court for the District of Nevada\" href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/U.S._District_Court_for_the_District_of_Nevada#Las_Vegas\">Lloyd D. US 95 connects the city to northwestern Nevada, including <a title=\"Carson City, Nevada\" href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carson_City,_Nevada\">Carson City</a> and <a class=\"mw-redirect\" title=\"Reno, NV\" href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reno,_NV\">Reno</a>. US 93 splits from I-15 northeast of Las Vegas and goes north through the eastern part of the state, serving <a title=\"Ely, Nevada\" href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ely,_Nevada\">Ely</a> and <a title=\"Wells, Nevada\" href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wells,_Nevada\">Wells</a>. US 95 heads south from US 93 near Henderson through far eastern California. A <a title=\"Las Vegas Beltway\" href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Las_Vegas_Beltway\">partial beltway</a> has been built, consisting of <a class=\"mw-redirect\" title=\"Interstate 215 (Nevada)\" href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Interstate_215_(Nevada)\">Interstate 215</a> on the south and <a class=\"mw-redirect\" title=\"Clark County 215\" href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Clark_County_215\">Clark County 215</a> on the west and north.
